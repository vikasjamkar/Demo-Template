Our Key Observations

Al is commoditizing analytics

We are at a pivotal junction of digital advancement wherein Artificial Intelligence is advancing exponentially, while the barriers to accessing this technological advancement is diminishing. Machine learning and Al tools are creating profound shifts in the data analytics landscape:

  • Accelerated velocity
  • Enhanced accuracy
  • Cost efficiencies
  • Predictive capabilities
  • Increased accessibility

We believe that Al tools will continue along the maturity curve reducing the reliance on human intervention in data analytics. Given that Advisory firms primarily differentiate themselves in their ability to deliver unique analytics through "human" expertise, these incumbents will be forced to re-design organizational structures, delivery models and commercial arrangements.

Advice is TOO theoretical

Today's institutions are facing unprecedented competition and disruption to their existing business models and in need for some much needed "practical" advice. However, majority of the "premier consultancies" find themselves unequipped to provide such practical advice due to lack of diversified operational expertise amongst their ranks. Instead, these institutions are primarily "selling" static or obsolete frameworks whereas the clients are demanding actionable insights.

The conventional commercial model for consulting where the advisory firms provide services to clients at little risk to its income stream continues to remain a source of frustration for the clients. The incumbent consulting firms often bear minimal ownership of delivery as primary risk of execution continues to reside with the client. For example, if the jointly designed strategies miss target deadlines, the engagement typically gets extended and the advisor benefits while the client bears the cost. This zero-sum commercial arrangement is ripe for an overhaul for high value strategic programs that bear significant risk for management.

Lastly, the incumbent consulting firms largely conduct engagement activities at an arms-length with limited client involvement. Consultancies prefer this approach to safeguard their proprietary methodologies constraining the client management teams' ability to learn and upskill during engagements. As such, this perpetuates a commercial cycle that allows these firms to address similar issues repeatedly without empowering the client to address these challenges in the future.
